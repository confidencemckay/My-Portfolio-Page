    About Me

    Education

    BCom: Economics and Informatics

    I am a BCom Economic Sciences (Economics & Informatics) graduate from North-West University, driven by the challenge of designing integrated systems that bridge the gap between technical infrastructure and economic strategy. My professional journey is rooted in a unique combination of "on-the-ground" commercial experience—beginning in my family’s Spaza shop—and high-level academic training in Information Systems and Economic Theory.

    Currently, I specialize in the development of automated quantitative trading systems. I leverage my background in IT and Economics to build and deploy trading bots that translate complex market indicators and trading strategies into executable logic. This work allows me to practice the core tenets of a Solutions Architect: designing robust frameworks, managing data integrity, and ensuring technical solutions align with financial goals.

    My long-term objective is to transition into Solutions Architecture, where I can design end-to-end ecosystems that solve complex organizational challenges. Whether I am analyzing data trends, optimizing software workflows, or managing economic risk, I approach every project with a focus on scalability, efficiency, and the strategic integration of technology.
